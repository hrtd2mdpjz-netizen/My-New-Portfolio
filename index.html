THE PROBLEM

Through my research of both the Indian and global digestive wellness market, I noticed that many products focused on a single benefit—such as fiber, probiotics, digestive enzymes, or hydration. While consumer awareness around gut health was growing, finding a simple, everyday solution that addressed multiple digestive wellness needs through clear, trustworthy communication was still challenging. This revealed an opportunity to rethink how gut health could be positioned for modern consumers.

THE SOLUTION

Rather than competing on exaggerated health claims, I focused on building a concept around simplicity, trust, and convenience. The goal was to create a premium daily gut wellness experience with clear positioning, approachable branding, and a product concept designed to support multiple aspects of digestive wellness without overwhelming the customer.

03 — MARKET RESEARCH

Understanding the opportunity before building the brand

Before making any branding or product decisions, I studied both the Indian and global digestive wellness market to understand consumer behaviour, category trends, product positioning, and communication styles. The objective wasn't to copy existing brands but to identify meaningful gaps where a differentiated concept could be created.

Key questions I explored:

  • How are leading global gut-health brands positioning themselves?
  • What problems are consumers actively trying to solve?
  • Which product categories dominate the market today?
  • Where are customers still experiencing friction?
  • What messaging builds trust instead of hype?

Global Consumer Shift

Growing awareness around digestive wellness and preventive health.

Category Gap

Many brands focus on individual digestive benefits rather than a broader daily wellness experience.

Communication Opportunity

Consumers increasingly prefer brands that educate clearly instead of relying on exaggerated health claims.

07 — PACKAGING STRATEGY

Translating the brand into a physical product

Packaging needed to signal trust and simplicity at shelf-level, in a category full of clinical or overly playful visual noise.

  • Sachet format for portability and daily-use framing
  • Dark navy base for a clean, trustworthy (not childish) feel
  • Flavor-coded accents: Lemon, Mixed Berry, Watermelon
  • Minimal ingredient callouts on-pack, full detail on website

16 — LESSONS LEARNED

What building GutClean taught me

1

Positioning is a discipline, not a slogan.

It took several rewrites before "simple, without overwhelm" actually fit the research and the persona.

2

Packaging has to work for both the shelf and the story.

Color and format choices had to serve trust, not just look nice.

3

AI speeds up drafts, but judgment still has to be mine.

The useful part of AI was volume and speed — the strategic decisions still needed to come from me.
